Life With New Devices 

On this page you will find scenarios that we might find ourselves in and examples of how our future smartphones may assist us.

The video above from YouTube channel BRIGHT SIDE depicts numerous concepts of how our smartphones may be in 2030, and applies examples of how the features may help users in daily life.

BCI to the Rescue

Thought controlled wheelchairs and prosthetic limbs are amazing examples of Brain Computer Interfacing today. BCI incorporated into our mobiles may have health benefits; for example, if alone and too ill to physically call an ambulance, the BCI on the mobile device could detect this and automatically phone an ambulance. Additionally, BCI within mobiles could be used for quick fixes when feeling pain, the device will show up the best solutions to help with the current situation. The BCI could take our health apps that we know today to a whole new dimension too; as well as reading our heart rate, the feature could also assist with dieting needs as well as warning you of posture improvements needed on an hourly basis. 

Dream Destination

Augmented reality (AR) is on the rise and in demand. Already used on mobiles in examples of Pokemon Go and Snapchat filters, our future could enhance this. The possibilities are endless but imagine an app that allowed you to briefly experience a holiday destination before booking, just to confirm whether you want to go or not. AR would virtually place you in the scenario showing you around the hotel or surrounding areas helping you decide whether to purchase or not! 

Locked Out?

In the imaginary scenario of forgetting your keys, in the future maybe this could no longer be a problem so long as we have our phones! Like some have their bank cards stored on their phone and can use this to scan at checkouts, in this concept we have our keys stored on our phone. Our doors would have to have a small scanning locking device on and in situations where we forget our physical keys, we can use this key 'card' on our phone to scan and suddenly we're no longer left out! To ensure security, we can use facial recognition from modern society to ensure the only person who has access to this key 'card' is the owner.
