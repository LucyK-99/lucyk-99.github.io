Here is a 2025 concept for a Samsung phone. The 'foldable' phones already made in today's society such as the Huawei Mate X have been criticised for being heavyweight and thick, so is the video above our solution along with better features? 

Flexi Future 

Long gone are the days we flip open our mobiles and make a call, or are they? With the 2020 release of Samsung's Galaxy Z Flip phone, this could be on the rise yet again and here to inspire our future. Whilst we know the conventional flip phone, we haven't yet got a fully flexible phone fit to turn into a watch, tablet or whatever size we desire. If you require a tablet AND a phone AND a watch, this concept could really benefit you! Just imagine the possibilities with a rolling or bendy screen and how travel friendly this would be!

MetamorphoNE

What was once just a future concept for mobiles seems to be turning more and more into reality! Firstly, we already have the newly released colour shifting Reno5 from Oppo which is innovative in itself. Released just this year in January 2021, it's the first of its kind and showcases a iridescent back that changes colour depending on the angle of the phone. This is thanks to thousands of refractive indices concentrated on the back making this possible.  

Whilst that is undoubtedly clever, it still doesn't offer us a different colour back for a set amount of time. But has Oppo just paved the way to allow this to happen in the future? Vivo, a technology company founded in China, previewed a prototype featuring a back that changes colour just by the switch of a button using electrochromic glass. Will this take off? Only time will tell...

Brain Wave

Already, we have the likes of Elon Musk developing his Link product but the drawback is it's invasive and involves a medical procedure into your head. Despite this, there are other non invasive products on the market which involve wearing head gear but NONE yet involving our handy mobile devices. If we already have the technology to develop Brain Computer Interfacing, surely it isn't beyond the realms of possibility to apply this to our phones. 

A phone working off of brain waves main feature would be that it has the ability to work without the user touching or speaking to the phone, essentially reading our minds. Whilst this raises privacy concerns and security issues, as long as you only telepathically tell your device the information you want it to know, is it really that bad then? We already input that information into our devices physically in today's age! Of course with this, the phone must have some defaults to avoid picking up unwanted information from your brain as well as being secure to your own mind- imagine the possibility of someone hacking your mind! Get in contact and let us know what you think here! 

Solar Power 

Solar systems are efficient renewable energy sources making them more environmentally friendly. They can be found in a range of places nowadays from running calculators, heating pools and providing electricity for a home to name just a few. With this in mind, it doesn't sound too ludicrous to suggest that one day in the future, phones may be solar powered!

Sounds great, right? Maybe not when the amount of charging time using solar energy is considered. Have you ever accidentally left your phone in the sun for too long and felt how overheated it is? This is another reason why solar energy can't work for us just yet; smart phones are designed to work up to a certain temperature until they result in breakage and complete data loss. Explore further with this helpful video from YouTube channel BRIGHT SIDE. 

Despite all of this, it may still be possible and hope has not been lost! It is crucial that our world becomes more sustainable moving forward therefore, solar powered phones have been given a rethought. To overcome the obstacles, the design and materials of smart phone can change to adapt to the heat. Additionally, rather than having a physical solar panel on the phone which doesn't seem practical, researchers and producers have thought a new designed thin film that transfers light into electricity may be the answer. The thin film can be inserted just below the display screen meaning maybe we are one step closer to a solar powered phone!

A Potted History of Our Mobile Phones  

1983-1995

Welcome to the Brick Phone 

1983 saw the first public release of a handheld device. The first mobile was the DynaTAC 8000X by Motorola with Nokia following suit with similar devices. They weighed over a kilogram and because of the weight, it was not strange to see people carrying them around in holsters! As if the phone wasn't big enough alone, they also had long aerials! 

1996-1999

The Rise of Communication 

Here we are met with the Nokia 9000 Communicator. The first to take a different physical approach to the phone. This one can be praised for paving the way with the keyboard design which still gets used in years to come ahead of 1996. The display screen plays a big part too looking how it has evolved from that to what our display screens look like now. 
*Just a fun fact FYI- it was a later model of this phone (the Nokia 9290 Communicator) which Kelly Rowland infamously text Nelly from using Excel in the Dilemma music video!*

2000-2002

The Nokia Era 

Who remembers playing snake on a Nokia? It is thanks to the year 2000 we were able to do this! Nokia released the Nokia 3310 phone that is undoubtedly a classic! This era is when the mobile became mainstream and finally they started to become everyday items compared to a luxury to what only some could afford prior to 2000. For several years came similar releases to this. This phone definitely helped the gaming market and aligns with what we know our mobiles to be today! 

2003-2007

FLIPping brilliant! 

Furthering the development of mobiles, here we see the clamshell phone. They have a complete different aesthetic compared to prior, now having a flip element. New developments also include a camera and vast connectivity such as Bluetooth which are still prominent aspects to our phones today! 

2008

Hello Touch Screen! 

Here is a design not so dissimilar to what we know in today's society! This is the LG Cookie, these type of phones brought music to our phones as well as a touch screen again paving the way to what we have today. The phone came with a stylus compared to just finger touch which is the preferred method nowadays. 

2009

BlackBerry Is Now Ripe

The BlackBerry became a cultural phenomenon for a number of years. The phone pictured is a Curve 8520 which bought back the use of a keyboard but also showcased a new track-pad feature.  The BlackBerry became popular for its wide range of applications especially that of BBM messaging service. After the popular 8520 came the Curve 9320 whose success was just as significant. After this 'phase', the now most common touchscreen phones came to the forefront... 

2010

Welcome Back Touch Screen

Pictured is the Samsung Galaxy S. This phone is the precursor to today's modern mobiles. Now the operating systems are becoming a big part of the device too e.g iOS and Android.

2020-

Our Mobile World Today 

The iPhone 12 Pro was released in late 2020. iPhone and Samsung are arguably the top mobile competitors with the majority of people owning a smartphone from either company. Now, phones with multiple cameras are becoming popularised as well as top spec features such as facial and fingerprint recognition. We use our phones for a variety of reasons not just for the main use of calling someone. Many of us wonder where we would be without them now as they are a part of our everyday routine!
